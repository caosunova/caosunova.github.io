The Augmentation Trap: Overreliance on Generative AI and the Productivity–Skill Trade-off
Michael S. Caosun, Sinan Aral

LLM tools can raise productivity on many information tasks, but cognitive offloading may erode worker skill over time. We develop a dynamic model of this tradeoff, decomposing AI productivity gains into a skill-neutral component and a skill-complementary component that scales with worker capability. Even fully rational, forward-looking workers can fall into an "augmentation trap": AI raises short-run output but erodes human capability, leaving long-run productivity lower than it would be without the tool.

The Negotiator's Dilemma at Scale: Value-Creating Interactions in AI-Mediated Negotiation
Michael Caosun, Jared Curhan, Sinan Aral

We simulate 3,000 negotiations between pairs of LLM-based agents, each assigned one of ten theory-grounded bargaining approaches, and approximate the payoff matrix for a multi-issue integrative scenario. The best approach pairing captures 64% of the integrative surplus, while every Nash equilibrium is competitive, capturing only 27%. Outcomes depend more on pairings than on individual approach quality, establishing the negotiator's dilemma in AI agents and quantifying the welfare cost of uncoordinated optimization.
