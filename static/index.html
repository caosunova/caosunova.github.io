I am a PhD student in the Information Technology Group at MIT Sloan studying how technology—especially AI—affects workers, markets, and organizations.

My current research focus is on modeling how AI augmentation changes task allocation and skill dynamics for workers. Previously, I used large language models to study negotiation.

I believe carefully designed experiments are one of the best tools for causal inference in organizational settings. I am actively seeking partnerships with firms willing to share data or collaborate on field studies of real AI workflows.

I previously studied mathematics and economics at the University of Chicago. Outside of research, I enjoy competitive EDH.
