I am a PhD candidate in the Information Technology group at MIT Sloan, advised by Sinan Aral.

I think about how AI adoption reshapes work and organizations. My research focuses on the feedback loops between AI systems and the people and institutions that use them — particularly where misaligned incentives cause short-run optimization to erode skills, distort markets, or reshape organizational capabilities in unintended ways. I use formal models, simulations, and large-scale experiments with LLM-based agents.

Before MIT, I studied mathematics and economics at the University of Chicago. An amateur interest in history, and the observation that technology tends to create inflection points for social change, is what brought me to this field. Outside of research, I enjoy playing strategic board games.
